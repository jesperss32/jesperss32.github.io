Food Prices in Correlation with Production Numbers

This page shows linear regression models for various products. Each graph has on the x-axis the production and on the y-axis the product price. Only products which have a significant correlation between production and their price are displayed.

These figures show that linear regression succeeds in detecting trends in foodprices based on the production data. Their neet to be taken in account that these predictions are based on very limited data which make them very subspectible to certain changes and outliers.

The following graphs shows correlations between change in food price and change in prodcution over continents. All the countries are assigned to their continent, and only combinations with a significant correlation are drawn.

Conclusion

Some regressions are based on very little data, this indicates a unstable relationship between products and prices.

What has to be noted that in contrary to the correlations found in countries, three out of four regional correlations appear to be negative. So food price drops as production increases.
